How Was Everything Created in the World of Technology?

Tech History - 16/01/2025

Ever stopped and thought, how the heck did all this even start? Why do computers exist? Who even came up with the idea of the internet, apps, video games, or that mysterious thing called code? It feels kinda like magic, right? But here’s the mind-blowing part, every complex creation we see today started from the simplest, most basic idea ever.

At the core of literally everything tech? 0 and 1. Yep, two tiny numbers flipping back and forth, controlling entire digital worlds. They’re not just numbers though, they represent on and off, yes and no, something and nothing. But why only two? Because at the most basic level, machines don’t think like us. They can only tell if something exists or doesn’t. A spark of energy, a pulse of electricity. And from that tiny pulse, we somehow ended up here, scrolling endlessly, streaming music, building entire virtual realities.

But who decided machines could even understand anything?

It all started when humans realized, “Hey, if we want to control a machine, we gotta talk its language.” That’s where programming languages came in. Not words like we use every day, but symbols, patterns, instructions. And you know what? It wasn’t some modern tech bro who first saw the potential. It was Ada Lovelace, the OG coder. She didn’t just see machines as calculators but imagined them as tools for creativity, where code could be used to make machines think.

And that’s where it hits different. Coding was never just about tech. It was about turning imagination into reality. Think about it. Music? It’s just notes and rhythm. Art? Colors and shapes. Code? Symbols and logic. And somehow, that string of characters and brackets can breathe life into entire digital experiences.

But why tho? Why does technology even exist? Maybe because humans have always been obsessed with breaking limits. From ancient storytelling on cave walls to writing algorithms on glowing screens, it’s all the same thing, really. Making thoughts tangible. Turning "what if" into "what is".

And maybe, just maybe, that’s the real magic. Tech wasn’t born just for efficiency. It was born from curiosity, from that craving to create, express, and connect. So next time you’re writing a line of code, opening a new app, or exploring some mind-blowing AI tool, remember, you’re not just using tech.

You’re literally part of that ancient quest to explore, to create, to keep asking...

How was all of this even created?
